Income Statement

Part 1: The Profit Machine

Ashki
The big picture! Income Statement and Balance Sheet 📋

What is an Income Statement?

An Income Statement (also called a Profit & Loss Statement) shows whether your business made a profit or a loss over a period of time — like a month, quarter, or year.

Think of it like a report card for your money: it tells you how much you earned, how much you spent, and what's left.

The Basic Formula

Sales − Cost of Sales = Gross Profit
Gross Profit − Operating Expenses = Net Profit

Breaking It Down

Sales (Revenue): All the money you made from selling products or services.

Cost of Sales: The direct costs to produce what you sold (e.g., materials, labour to make products).

Gross Profit: Sales minus Cost of Sales. This shows profit before operating expenses.

Operating Expenses: Costs to run the business (rent, electricity, salaries, marketing, etc.).

Net Profit: Your final profit — what's left after all expenses. This flows into owner's equity!

💡 In Real Life: Zandile's spaza shop made R4,200 in sales last month. Her Cost of Sales was R2,100 (stock she bought to resell). Her Gross Profit was R2,100. Then she paid R1,400 in expenses (rent, electricity, delivery), leaving her with R700 net profit!

Balance Sheet

Part 2: The Financial Position

What is a Balance Sheet?

A Balance Sheet shows what your business owns and owes at a specific point in time — like December 31st. It's a financial photograph, not a movie.

It follows the fundamental accounting equation: Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity

The Three Sections

Assets: What your business owns (cash, inventory, equipment, vehicles, property)

Liabilities: What your business owes (loans, supplier invoices you haven't paid, rent owing)

Owner's Equity: What's left for the owner after all debts are paid. This includes opening equity + net profit from the Income Statement.

The Key Difference

Income Statement: A PERIOD of time (Jan–Dec 2024)

Balance Sheet: A POINT in time (As at 31 Dec 2024)

💡 In Real Life: On 31 Dec, Zandile's spaza shop had R15,000 in total assets (cash, stock, equipment). She owed R10,000 to her supplier. So her owner's equity was R5,000 (R15,000 − R10,000). That R700 profit from the Income Statement gets added to her equity!
Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity
R15,000 = R10,000 + R5,000 ✓ It balances!
