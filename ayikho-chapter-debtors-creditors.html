Debtors Journal

2 of 10

What is a Debtors Journal?

The Debtors Journal (DJ) records all credit sales—when you sell goods and the customer promises to pay later.

💰 Key Concept:

When you sell on credit, the customer becomes a DEBTOR. They owe you money.

Columns in a Debtors Journal:

Column What it means
Date When the sale happened
Debtor Name Who bought from you
Folio Page reference (advanced)
Sales Amount before VAT
Cost of Sales How much it cost you
VAT 15% tax (South Africa)
📚 Exam Tip

Remember: VAT in SA is 15%. If you sell for R100, you add R15 VAT, so total is R115.

Why it matters:

In Zandile's spaza shop, tracking debtors is CRUCIAL. She needs to know who owes her money so she can follow up for payment!

Creditors Journal

4 of 10

What is a Creditors Journal?

The Creditors Journal (CJ) records all credit purchases—when you buy goods and promise to pay the supplier later.

💳 Key Concept:

When you buy on credit, the supplier becomes a CREDITOR. You owe them money.

The relationship:

DEBTORS ← YOU → CREDITORS

  • Debtors: Owe money TO you (you gave them goods)
  • Creditors: You owe money TO them (they gave you goods)

Columns in a Creditors Journal:

Column What it means
Date When you bought the goods
Creditor Name Who you bought from
Folio Page reference
Purchases Amount before VAT
VAT 15% tax (South Africa)
📚 Exam Tip

CJ is almost like the opposite of DJ! Instead of tracking who owes you, you track who you owe.

Date Creditor Purchases VAT Total

🎯 The Big Picture:

Zandile sold R1,250 on credit (3 debtors) and bought R1,300 on credit (2 creditors). She needs to manage both to keep her spaza shop running smoothly!

Question 1 of 3

Thandi buys groceries from Zandile's spaza shop and promises to pay next week. What is Thandi in relation to Zandile?

A) A creditor (because she will pay later)
B) A debtor (because she owes money to Zandile)
C) A supplier (because she buys from Zandile)
D) A customer on cash basis (not relevant to credit)
✓ Correct! Thandi bought on credit, so she owes Zandile money—making her a debtor. Zandile would record this in her Debtors Journal.
💡 Memory Trick:

Debtor = Dues to You | Creditor = Credit given by them

Quiz Time!

Question 2 of 3

Zandile buys stock from City Wholesale on credit (30 days payment terms). Which journal should she use to record this?

A) Sales Journal
B) Debtors Journal
C) Creditors Journal
D) Cash Receipts Journal
✓ Correct! City Wholesale is a creditor (Zandile owes them money). Credit purchases go in the Creditors Journal, not the Debtors Journal.
📝 Key Difference:

Debtors Journal: When YOU make the sale | Creditors Journal: When YOU make the purchase

Question 3 of 3

If Zandile sells goods worth R800 (before VAT) on credit, what is the total amount including VAT that should be recorded?

A) R800
B) R880
C) R920
D) R1,000
✓ Correct! R800 + 15% VAT (R120) = R920 total. Remember: 15% is SA's standard VAT rate. Quick calc: R800 × 1.15 = R920.
🧮 VAT Formula:

Total = Amount × 1.15 (adds 15% VAT in one step) | Or: Amount × 0.15 = VAT amount
